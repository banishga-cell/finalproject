Game of the Month

Every month, I highlight a game that captured my attention and deserves your time. It may be a game that just came out, a game that's 20 years old; it may be a VR game or an exclusive to a niche, impossible-to-get console from 15 years ago. I try to keep the picks unique, meaningful, and ones that can resonate with my audience. My picks are not for everyone. But I can promise that no matter what, they will be games that deserves someone's attention here.

Released: November 11th, 2008

The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim

RPG Open World Story-Rich

The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim remains one of the most captivating open-world experiences ever created. Its sprawling landscapes—snow-covered peaks, ancient ruins, and bustling cities—invite endless exploration, rewarding players with secrets tucked into every corner of the map. Thus, to little surprise, I have found myself in Tamriel once again this month.

The freedom to forge your own identity, whether as a master thief, powerful mage, or wandering adventurer, makes every playthrough feel uniquely personal. It's a world that players don't just visit—they live in.
